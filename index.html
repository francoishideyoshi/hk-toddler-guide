Rainy-day / typhoon shortlist

Rainy season runs Apr–Sep (June worst for rainstorm warnings); typhoon season peaks Jul–Sep. Air-conditioned fallback: Baumhaus / EpicLand / Kiztopia indoor playrooms, HK Science Museum (under-4 free), Central Library Toy Library, indoor heated pools with water playgrounds (e.g. Sai Wan Ho), and the Hopewell Centre kids' floor (Wan Chai). Free under age 3

Playgroups

Most playgroups at 13–24 months are parent-accompanied. Prices change often — confirm directly before enrolling.

Victoria Playpark: no admissions priority

Victoria Playpark's own FAQ states students receive no priority admission to VEO kindergartens — everyone follows the same procedure and admission isn't guaranteed. The benefit is familiarity with VEO's trilingual/inquiry style, not a queue-jump. Source: victoriaplaypark.com/faq.

The "feeder" reality

Schools with a full playgroup→pre-nursery→kindergarten pathway often give their own playgroup graduates priority or an interview bypass (e.g. after 3+ months at the affiliated playgroup) — but this is case-by-case, not universal (Victoria explicitly does not). Playgroup-only centres are not EDB-regulated; pre-nursery/kindergartens must be. Verify per school. Sources: letsgethome.com, Mighty Oaks Acorn.

Education: The Decision

Local/bilingual vs. international kindergarten — cost, language, pathway to primary, and how easy it is to switch later.

Local / bilingual track

Annual cost
KES-subsidised half-day: tuition often free + ~HK$200–500/month misc fees. Whole-day/long full-day: subsidy doesn't fully cover, top-up likely. Non-KES private local: ~HK$2,000–6,000/month. exact 2025/26 KES rates unconfirmed
Language
Cantonese-first, plus Putonghua and English at bilingual schools.
Pathway to primary
Government/aided P1 places via POA (Primary One Admission): Discretionary Places stage (1 school; auto-in for siblings/staff, else points), then Central Allocation (school-net based, part choice/part random). Most local KGs are not linked to a specific primary — KG choice doesn't guarantee a P1 place. feeder links unconfirmed at school level
Flexibility to switch
Easiest early (PN/K1). Moving local→international later means competing for scarce mid-year seats and an English gap.

International track

Annual cost
~HK$50,000 to >HK$200,000/year; a Tier-1 first year with one-off fees can exceed HK$230,000.
Language
English-dominant; some 50/50 English-Mandarin immersion models.
Pathway to primary
Outside POA — apply directly. ESF's new Associated Schools Model (from 2026) tells K1 families their linked primary and guarantees a Y1 place on completing K2 (must enrol before 1 Dec) — the strongest guaranteed pipeline found in this research. Victoria (VEO) → Victoria Shanghai Academy: 694 VEO grads got VSA offers AY2021/22–2024/25 (strong track record, not guaranteed). Malvern Pre-School → Malvern College HK Prep 1 after 2+ years (subject to admissions).
Flexibility to switch
International→local later means a Cantonese/Chinese-literacy gap to close.

What is KES?

The Kindergarten Education Scheme ("Free Quality Kindergarten Education") replaced the old voucher scheme in 2017/18. EDB pays the subsidy directly to eligible non-profit local kindergartens — about 730 KGs (~75% of all HK KGs) participate, across all 18 districts. Eligibility requires the child hold an HKID or valid student visa exact wording unconfirmed. Fee remission for low-income families (KCFRS + Grant-KG) is available on top of KES via WFSFAA. Source: EDB KES overview.

Debentures & capital levies, explained

A debenture is a one-off lump sum for priority admission (can be corporate, nominating an employee's child, or individual/family-held, sometimes partially refundable). A capital levy is the annual alternative — non-refundable, may total the same or more over time. At the pre-school/nursery level, most schools do not require either.

Examples found: ESF Individual Nomination Rights HK$500,000 (each, primary/secondary), kindergarten bond from HK$50,000 (converts to a Year 1 nomination right); Nord Anglia annual capital levy HK$35,000 for new students from 2026/27; Guidepost Montessori explicitly charges none. General market range for a capital levy: HK$25,000–45,000/year; debentures HK$25,000 (ESF-tier) up to millions at top primary/secondary schools.

  3. November 2026

    Interviews / play assessments

    Rarely a formal 1-on-1 at this age — schools run play/activity carousels observing instruction-following, motor skills, and language. Parents are often briefly interviewed too.

  4. September 2027

    PN starts (age 2y3m)

    PN/N1 is optional and is not part of the EDB-regulated kindergarten curriculum — it does not materially affect K1 admission at most schools (Victoria explicitly grants no priority from it).

  5. 1–30 September 2027

    ESF K1 application window Fixed dates

    ESF's online K1 application window is fixed every year: opens 1 Sept, closes 30 Sept, for entry the following September (here, Sept 2028). Applications after 30 Sept go to a date-ordered waitlist tied to the first-preference school only.

  6. Autumn 2027 (Sept–Nov)

    Local K1 applications

    Apply for the EDB "Registration Certificate for Kindergarten Admission" (6–8 week processing) alongside school applications; interviews in November, results late Nov–early Dec, registration early-mid January.

  7. September 2028

    K1 starts (age 3y3m)

    This child, born June 2025, is eligible: the 2028/29 K1 cutoff requires birth on or before 31 December 2025.
